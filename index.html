Rules

v0.1.0

Goal

  • The goal of this game is to attack and destroy your opponentâ€™s Kings
  • Once a king takes 10 or more damage, it is flipped over
  • If a King takes 10 or more damage while flipped over, it is destroyed

Setup

  • 2-4 players
  • Each player selects a king and puts it in front of them face-up
  • Jacks, Queens, and Aces are shuffled and put in a separate pile
  • Optionally, shuffle in Jokers and/or extra Kings with the other face cards
  • Shuffle the rest of the deck and deal 5 cards to each player

Gameplay

This game consists of rounds
During a round, any player can make a move at any time

Attacking
  • A player can only take damage once per turn
  • The player takes a Spade or Club card out of his/her hand and places it in front of the King they wish to attack
  • The defender can choose to heal before taking damage, take the damage, heal the attack, or counterattack
  • When taking damage, the damage taken is equal to the number on the attacking card
  • If the damage taken causes the King to have over 10 damage, it is flipped over and all damage is cleared
  • If it is already flipped over, it is destroyed
  • After taking damage, if the King is not flipped over or destroyed, the defender can immediately heal using any heart cards in their hand
  • If the player chooses to heal the attack, he/she can play a heart card from their hand and take damage equal to the difference between the attacking number and healing number
  • If the healing number is equal to or greater than the attacking number, no damage is taken or healed
  • NOTE: IF NO DAMAGE IS TAKEN, THE PLAYER CAN BE ATTACKED AGAIN IN THE SAME ROUND
  • If the player chooses to counterattack, they can play a Spade or Club from their own hand greater than or equal to the number of the attacking card
  • If the counterattack number is equal to the attacking number, no damage is taken
  • If the counterattack number is greater than the attacking number, the defender then attacks the attacker with an attack equal to the the counterattack
  • All attacking/defending cards are discarded after being played
Healing
  • A player can heal as much as they want at any time during the round
  • Playing a heart card heals the player by the number on the card
  • Normal heart cards cannot flip up a flipped-over King
  • The Heart card is discarded after being played
Buying
  • A player can buy as much as they want at any time during the round
  • Discarding Diamonds whose values add up to 10 or more allows the player to draw one card from the special card pile
  • After drawing, the player looks at it and either uses it or places it face-down in front of their King
After the Round
  • Players may discard unwanted cards
  • Shuffle discard piles for deck and special cards if needed
  • All players draw cards until they have 5 cards in their hands
  • Start the next round

Card Descriptions

Non-Face Cards
  • Spades/Clubs
    • Used for Attacking
  • Hearts
    • Used for Healing
  • Diamonds
    • Used for Buying Special Cards
Special Cards
  • Queens
    • All Queens heal 10 health
    • Using a Queen while full health but flipped over flips you back face-up
  • Jacks
    • Jack of Spades deals 10 damage, unhealable by hearts (can be blocked by queen/healed by Jack of Hearts)
    • Jack of Clubs destroys all Queens of the targeted player
    • Jack of Hearts fully heals your King and flips it face-up if it is flipped over
    • Jack of Diamonds requires each player to pay a 10-diamond tax before they can attack the player who played it
  • Aces
    • Once an Ace is flipped face-up, the player that own it can place cards of the same suit as the Ace on top of the Ace, freeing up space in their hand for more cards
    • A player can attack using more than one card at a time if all cards are on top of the same Ace
    • For example, if a player has the Ace of spades and has the 6 and 7 of Spades stacked on it, he/she can attack with both at once for a 13 damage attack
    • In dire situations, a player can use the Ace - it counts as a 10 of whatever suit the Ace is
  • Extra Kings and Jokers that are optionally added to the deck do nothing

Tips

  • Avoid healing an entire attack if possible - this allows you to be attacked again in the same round - try to take damage first and heal after, or use a Heart card with a lower number than the attack to take little damage
  • If you are flipped over and have a Queen, prioritize healing yourself to full and using the Queen at full HP to flip face-up as fast as possible
  • Aces are very strong - the Ace of Diamonds in particular. Try hoarding all the Diamonds so no one can buy any special cards
  • Save the Jack of Hearts until facing an attack that will destroy you and you have no other way of defending
  • When you draw a King or Joker, play it like any normal special card face down behind your King as a bluff
